What is Disciplined Thought?

Disciplined Thought is a system of thinking for acquiring accurate beliefs and making effective decisions.

Many books and courses about "critical thinking" and "skepticism" focus on how to debunk false claims. This is not a positive and constructive approach. Without a guide to finding positive concepts, we could engage in disproof forever and not find a single decent idea.

It isn't hopeless. There are simple and effective ways to tell beneficial ideas from harmful ones.

Observe

There are two types of observations.

Measurements are representations of a single property.

A fact is a measurement confirmed by multiple independent observers.

Identifications are for when a collection of measurements matches a definition.

This is a triangle:

For an identification to become a fact it not only needs independent corroboration, but must also use a proper definition.

A proper definition must:

  • Be stated in a clear and unambiguous way
  • Rely only on facts
  • Have no significant false examples
  • Have no significant true examples that don't fit the definition
  • Be as simple as possible

Understand

We should strive to have accurate beliefs so the world we imagine is as close as possible to the real world.

A belief is a rule used to model our world.

An accurate belief must be:

  • Based on at least one fact
  • Logically and unambiguously stated
  • Predicts a falsifiable observation
  • Falsifying observations have not yet been made

Overcome

We can use factual observations and accurate beliefs to solve problems and conquer the challenges blocking us from our goals.

Problem solving usually includes these steps:

  1. Observe the problem and define it clearly.
  2. Limit range of possible solutions.
  3. Select one possible solution.
  4. Predict the solution's outcome.
  5. Evaluate the quality of the outcome.
  6. After an appropriate number of solutions are evaluated, decide on the best.
